Designing Gumroad

Product Designer – May 2014 to October 2015

A breakdown of my approach to three core features focused on helping creatives of all types make a living online while leading product design at Gumroad.

  • Onboarding
  • Audience
  • Discover

Onboarding new users Creators

Since it's inception in 2011, Gumroad's feature set had grown extensively beyond "a way to ask folks to pay for downloads" and in early 2015 we began exposing more of the awesome things we had built for our creators. One of the ways this was accomplished, was via a system of interstitial views designed by myself and built in collaboration with @amiruci and @shl aptly named "Greeters". From gathering information, to generating understanding and excitement for a given feature, Greeters were a simple & straight-forward method for making new creators more successful, faster. Here's a look at what that experience was like.

Growing an audience with Gumroad

Gumroad since it's begining has been an excellent e-commerce tool for those that already have an audience but for those who don't, our creators were largely left to their own devices. Begining in July of 2015, I worked on a suite of improvements designed to make profiles simple, high-converting pages where new creators could collect email addresses while they worked on their publishing their first product on Gumroad, and in turn, make their first dollar using the service.
